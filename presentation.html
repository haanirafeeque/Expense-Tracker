The Problem

Most people don't know
where their money goes

Sound familiar? These are the most common money struggles.

😰

No Visibility

You spend but never track, so you're always surprised at the end of the month.

📊

No Budgets

Without limits per category, overspending on food or shopping is easy to miss.

📝

Messy Records

Spreadsheets are tedious. Receipts get lost. Memory is unreliable.

📱

Complex Apps

Existing finance apps are overwhelming, require sign-ups, or cost money.

The Solution

Meet TrackSpend

TrackSpend is a free, browser-based expense tracker. No sign-up. No app download. Just open it and start logging your expenses.

It gives you a clear picture of your spending with charts, budget warnings, and a clean history log — all saved automatically in your browser.

Features

Everything you need,
nothing you don't

📊

Live Dashboard

See your total spending, number of transactions, and top spending category at a glance.

🥧

Spending Pie Chart

A visual chart breaks down your spending by category so you instantly see where money goes.

🎯

Budget Goals

Set a budget limit for each category. Progress bars and warnings alert you when you overspend.

➕

Add & Edit Expenses

Log any expense with a description, amount, category, and date. Edit or delete at any time.

🔍

Search & Filter

Quickly find expenses by searching descriptions or filtering by category.

💾

Auto-Save

All your data is automatically saved in the browser. Close the tab and it's still there when you return.

Takeaways

What this project taught me

01

SPA Routing

How AngularJS loads different "pages" without ever refreshing the browser — all content is injected into one shell page.

02

MVC Architecture

Keeping data (Service), logic (Controller), and display (View) separate makes code easier to read and debug.

03

Browser Storage

Using localStorage to persist data means users can close the app and come back without losing anything — no server needed.

04

UI/UX Design

Good design isn't just looks — it's about making actions clear, feedback immediate, and the experience intuitive.
